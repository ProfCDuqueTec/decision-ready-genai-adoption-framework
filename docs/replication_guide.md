# Replication Guide

This guide describes how to reuse and adapt the Decision-Ready GenAI Adoption Framework in a new institutional context.

## Step 1. Review local purpose

Clarify whether your objective is:
- research publication,
- internal institutional diagnosis,
- curriculum redesign,
- or comparative replication.

## Step 2. Adapt the instrument

Review:
- linguistic fit,
- local terminology,
- AI policy context,
- and the relevance of academic activity categories.

Preserve the conceptual meaning of:
- Performance Expectancy,
- Effort Expectancy,
- Social Influence,
- Facilitating Conditions,
- and usage-frequency items.

## Step 3. Secure ethical alignment

Before data collection:
- obtain local ethics approval if needed;
- prepare informed-consent language;
- avoid unnecessary identifiers;
- define data storage and protection procedures.

## Step 4. Collect data transparently

If probability sampling is not feasible:
- describe the recruitment process clearly;
- report participation conditions transparently;
- avoid overclaiming representativeness.

## Step 5. Structure the dataset

Follow the variable naming conventions provided in:
- `templates/data_dictionary.csv`

## Step 6. Run the analysis

Recommended order:
1. construct descriptives
2. item-level asymmetries
3. HC3-robust OLS
4. segment analysis
5. export tables

## Step 7. Interpret contextually

Do not interpret results in isolation from local conditions.
Pay particular attention to:
- infrastructure variation,
- policy clarity,
- faculty signalling,
- and alignment with assessment practices.

## Step 8. Report responsibly

When writing up findings:
- distinguish local substantive results from methodological transferability;
- report limitations clearly;
- avoid causal claims not supported by the design.

## Suggested output logic

A useful reporting structure is:
- context and motivation,
- instrument and design,
- construct-level results,
- item-level friction analysis,
- robust regression,
- segmentation,
- implications for action.
