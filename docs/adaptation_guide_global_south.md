# Adaptation Guide for Global South and Resource-Heterogeneous Contexts

This guide supports adaptation of the framework to contexts where infrastructure, policy maturity, and institutional support may vary across programmes or campuses.

## Why contextual adaptation matters

In resource-heterogeneous settings, adoption patterns may be shaped not only by student beliefs, but also by:
- connectivity conditions,
- hardware access,
- platform availability,
- faculty norms,
- institutional signalling,
- and local policy clarity.

## Recommended adaptation principles

### 1. Preserve conceptual structure
Keep the analytic core stable:
- PE
- EE
- SI
- FC
- usage-frequency indicators

### 2. Localise wording carefully
Adjust terms related to:
- generative AI,
- coursework,
- institutional support,
- and responsible use

without changing the underlying meaning.

### 3. Treat infrastructure as interpretive context
Do not assume infrastructure is neutral.
It may shape both Facilitating Conditions and Social Influence.

### 4. Pay close attention to integration effort
In many contexts, the most important friction may be workflow integration rather than tool learnability.

### 5. Avoid one-size-fits-all implementation
Segment-based interpretation may be especially valuable in heterogeneous systems.

## Practical recommendation

If resources are limited, begin with:
- the core instrument,
- construct descriptives,
- item-level diagnostics,
- and one robust regression model.

Segmentation can be added afterward.

## Final note

The goal of adaptation is not to reproduce wording mechanically.
The goal is to preserve analytic comparability while respecting local reality.
