# Administration Guidance

This document provides practical guidance for administering the reusable survey instrument.

## Recommended administration conditions

- Use a clear informed-consent statement.
- State that participation is voluntary.
- Do not collect direct identifiers unless local approval explicitly allows it.
- If possible, prevent duplicate submissions.
- Include one attention-check item.
- Keep the survey concise and easy to complete.

## Adaptation recommendations

When adapting the instrument:
- preserve the conceptual meaning of each construct;
- avoid changing item polarity unless necessary;
- align terminology with local academic language;
- review the wording of “generative AI” based on local familiarity.

## Ethical alignment

Before deployment:
- verify local ethics requirements;
- review institutional AI guidelines;
- ensure participants understand how data will be used;
- clarify whether results will be used for research, internal diagnostics, or both.

## Reporting recommendations

When publishing or presenting:
- describe the sampling strategy transparently;
- avoid overclaiming external validity;
- distinguish between local findings and transferable methodological elements;
- report contextual constraints clearly.
